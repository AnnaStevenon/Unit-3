Wisconsin Agricultural Production in 2022



What are these units?

In the United States, agricultural yield is commonly given in bushels per acre for row crops (i.e., corn grain, soybeans, wheat, and oats) and in US tons per acre for other agricultural products such as corn stover. The bushel is a unit of volume which denotes a different total mass for individual goods. A bushel is equal to 8 US dry gallons.


According to the U.S. Grains Council:

  • Corn grain: 54 lbs per bushel.

  • Wheat & soybeans: 60 lbs per bushel.

  • Oats: 32 lbs per bushel.

  • Corn silage: 1 US ton = 2000 lbs.

*These data were collected from the USDA Quick Stats Database.
**Note that a value of zero indicates unpublishable data and does not indicate a crop yield or animal count of zero.
